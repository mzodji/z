üîé Research overview

My doctoral research focuses on free boundary problems for models describing the flow of fluid mixtures. The constituents of the mixture are assumed to be immiscible (for instance, gas bubbles in a liquid) and are separated by one or more interfaces of zero thickness. The objective is to investigate the propagation of the regularity of these interfaces over time; this involves establishing the well-posedness of the underlying models. We employ Hoff-type energy estimates together with Lagrangian changes of variables and a number of harmonic analysis tools.

Currently, I study a fluid‚Äìstructure interaction model, and the regularity of Lagrangian trajectories for solutions of the incompressible Euler equations. Both projects draw on the theory of Littlewood‚ÄìPaley and on microlocal analysis.
